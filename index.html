Thesis

A project that ties analysis, simulation, and testing together

Aerodynamic Modeling, Experimental Validation, and Dynamic Analysis of a Spherical Sailing Omnidirectional Rover (SSAILOR)

This thesis evaluates SSailOR, a lightweight spherical rover that uses a dual-sail configuration and traction hoop to achieve controlled, wind-driven mobility. The broader goal is long-duration traversal in remote and harsh environments such as planetary surfaces and Arctic regions.

The work combines a dynamic model formulated using Kane's method, wind tunnel testing, CFD in ANSYS Fluent, a Weissinger-based vortex lattice implementation, and XFLR5 cross-checks to understand how sail angles, yaw angle, geometry changes, and coupled system dynamics affect lift, drag, stability, and maneuverability.

Dynamics Project

Modeling and Simulation of a Flapping Wing Micro Air Vehicle (FWMAV)

I developed a multibody dynamics model for a flapping wing micro air vehicle, focusing on body frames, wing kinematics, generalized coordinates, and simulated trajectory response.

In practical terms, this project was about understanding how a very small bio-inspired flying system moves, so its flapping behavior and resulting flight trajectory could be predicted and studied before any hardware is built or tested.
